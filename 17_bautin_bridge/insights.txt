  What is search, physically?

  Search is: random parameter configurations are sampled, and we ask what
  fraction lands in a "viable" region of parameter space. S(d) = 1/P(viable)

  For cusp, "viable" means: the 1D drift f(x) has three zeros (two stable, one

  is a wedge. As d grows, random parameters map into effective (a,b) coordinates
   via a many-to-few projection, and the central limit theorem concentrates the
  projection away from the wedge. Hence exponential decay.

  For Bautin, "viable" means: a 2D system has a stable FP coexisting with a
  stable limit cycle. This requires complex eigenvalues + the right nonlinear
  structure (subcritical Hopf). It's a condition on the Jacobian spectrum AND
  higher-order terms.

  The concentration-of-measure argument for cusp works because the viable region
